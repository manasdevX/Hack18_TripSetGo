# Goa Travel Guide

## Overview
Goa is India's smallest state and a top tourist destination known for its beaches, Portuguese architecture, and vibrant nightlife. It sits on the western coast of India (Konkan coast). Best time to visit: November to February.

## Top Beaches

### Baga Beach
- One of the most popular beaches in North Goa.
- Excellent for water sports: jet skiing (₹800/ride), banana boat (₹400/person), parasailing (₹1,200).
- Shacks open from 8 AM to midnight.
- Nearest town: Calangute (2 km).

### Calangute Beach
- Known as the "Queen of Beaches."
- Very crowded during December and January.
- Beach shacks serve seafood: fish thali ₹250, lobster ₹800, prawn curry ₹350.
- Water sports hub: multiple operators at ₹500–₹1,500 per activity.

### Palolem Beach
- South Goa's most scenic crescent-shaped beach.
- Quieter and more relaxed than North Goa.
- Popular for kayaking (₹300/hr) and dolphin trips (₹400/person).
- Beach hut accommodation: ₹1,500–₹3,500 per night.

### Anjuna Beach
- Famous flea market every Wednesday (8 AM – 6 PM).
- Best for budget travelers; lots of cheap guesthouses starting at ₹600/night.
- Nightlife: Curlies Beach Shack, entry free, cocktails from ₹200.

## Must-Visit Attractions

- **Basilica of Bom Jesus** (Old Goa): UNESCO site, free entry, open 9 AM – 6:30 PM.
- **Fort Aguada** (Candolim): 17th-century Portuguese fort, entry ₹25, great sunset views.
- **Dudhsagar Falls**: 310m waterfall, jeep safari from Mollem ₹2,200–₹2,500 per jeep (up to 6 people).
- **Calangute Market**: Local spices, cashews, handicrafts.
- **Chapora Fort** (Vagator): Made famous by Dil Chahta Hai, free entry.
- **Saturday Night Market** (Arpora): Weekly market, entry ₹100, live music, 200+ stalls.

## Restaurants & Food

- **Fisherman's Wharf** (Cavelossim): Seafood, mains ₹400–₹1,200.
- **Britto's** (Baga): Iconic beachside restaurant, fish curry ₹350, prawn masala ₹500.
- **Curlies** (Anjuna): Famous beach shack, cocktails ₹200, grilled fish ₹400.
- **Café Bodega** (Panjim): Heritage café, breakfast thali ₹180, cold coffee ₹120.
- **Venite Restaurant** (Panjim): Oldest restaurant in Goa since 1936, Goan sausage ₹280.

## Transport

- **Flights to Goa**: Dabolim Airport (GOI) receives flights from all major Indian cities.
  - Mumbai to Goa: 1h, ₹2,500–₹5,000 round trip (IndiGo, Air India).
  - Delhi to Goa: 2.5h, ₹4,000–₹9,000 round trip.
  - Bangalore to Goa: 1h, ₹2,000–₹4,500 round trip.
- **Trains**: Konkan Railway connects Goa to Mumbai (Mandovi Express, 11h).
- **Local transport**: 
  - Bikes on rent: ₹300–₹500/day (Honda Activa).
  - Taxis (goa cabs): ₹300–₹600 for airport transfers (North Goa).
  - Auto rickshaws: ₹100–₹250 within towns.

## Travel Tips
- Avoid May–September (monsoon season; most beaches and shacks closed).
- Book accommodation 2–3 months in advance for Christmas and New Year (December 20–January 5).
- Carry cash; many small shacks and taxis don't accept cards.
- Respect local culture at churches and temples (cover shoulders and knees).
- Best cashews in India: Mapusa market, 1 kg for ₹500–₹700.
